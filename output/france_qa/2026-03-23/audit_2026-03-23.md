## 5. Anomaly Report

### 5.1 MAR R3 — Extremely Low Figures (4–20)

**Issue:** Winner KIAORA DU SOLEIL finished in 144.20s for 2000m (pace: 14.50 s/f).
Standard time for MAR Turf 2000m is 133.15s. The deviation is 9.56s = 47.8 lengths
= 83.6 lbs, producing a raw figure of just 16.4.

**Root cause:** This is almost certainly an **Arabian race** that was classified
as PLAT (flat) by PMU. Arabian thoroughbreds race ~8-12% slower than regular
thoroughbreds at the same distance. All 14 runners are aged 4, and horse names
(KIAORA DU SOLEIL, HAFIDA AL CHAM, AMBRA SAINT LON) are consistent with Arabian
naming conventions.

**Impact:** 11 runners rated 4–20 (correctly low given they ran against TB
standard times). The figures are internally consistent but should not be compared
with TB figures.

**Resolution:** Added QC-4 check to the pipeline — winners finishing >7% slower
than standard time (after GA correction) are now flagged as likely Arabian/non-TB
races. MAR R3 is correctly excluded with this check (7.2% deviation detected).
This also would have caught the A33 R9 issue from March 22.

### 5.2 MAR R5 — Beaten Horse Rated Higher Than Winner

**Issue:** LAUPER (2nd, fig 89) rated higher than winner GARRIS (1st, fig 83).

**Explanation:** This is EXPECTED and correct behavior. LAUPER carried 141 lbs
(+15.1 lbs weight adjustment) while GARRIS carried 127 lbs (+0.8 lbs).
LAUPER finished 2.00L behind but gets a +14.3 lbs net weight credit, which
more than compensates for the 2-length beaten margin (~6.6 lbs). This is a
standard handicap race outcome — the weight adjustment correctly identifies
LAUPER as the higher-ability horse despite finishing second.

**Status: NOT A BUG** — working as designed.
