## Issue 1 — CRITICAL: SAI Race 2 (900m Turf) produces absurd figures (179–214)

**Race:** SAI Race 2, 900m, Très souple, Turf, Class 5, 7 runners

| Horse | Pos | Raw Figure | Calibrated |
|---|---|---|---|
| RAFAGA | 1 | 294.36 | **214.11** |
| OPIUM OF SUCCESS | 2 | 269.90 | **197.23** |
| NAAD'IN | 3 | 262.37 | **192.04** |
| THE BROTHER | 7 | 241.10 | **179.65** |

**Root cause:** The winner's finishing time of **53.71s** for 900m is massively below the standard time of **73.14s** (a 19.43s differential). The going allowance is **negative** (-0.0101 s/m), which for "Très souple" (very soft) ground is physically impossible — soft ground should slow horses down, not speed them up.

**Calculation trace:**
- `corrected_time = 53.71 - (-0.0101 × 900) = 62.81s`
- `deviation = 62.81 - 73.14 = -10.33s` → runner deemed 10s faster than standard
- `raw_figure = 100 + 194.36 = 294.36`

**Diagnosis:** Either the finishing time (53.71s) or the standard time (73.14s) for SAI 900m Turf is wrong. A 900m race on very soft ground in ~54s implies ~16.7 m/s which is world-class sprint speed on fast ground. The 73s standard equates to ~12.3 m/s which seems too slow for 900m. Most likely: **the standard time for SAI 900m Turf is derived from too few samples and/or contaminated data**, or the finishing time from PMU is incorrect.

Additionally, the negative GA for "Très souple" at SAI on this day contradicts the GA prior of +0.52 s/f. The real-time GA calculation was likely distorted by this very race's bad data.

**Recommendation:** Flag or suppress figures for any race where `|raw_figure| > 150` or where the going allowance sign contradicts the going description (negative GA on soft/heavy ground).

**Root cause:** The going description is **empty** for all AVE races. The `going` field is blank, which likely causes the pipeline to skip figure computation for this race (possibly treating it as missing data rather than falling back to the `""` GA prior of 0.10 s/f which exists in `constants.py`).

AVE Race 3 at the same course/distance *does* produce figures (with the same empty going), so the skip may be **class-dependent** or related to a missing artifact key.

**Recommendation:** Investigate why AVE R1 is skipped when AVE R3 at the same course/distance/surface is not. Ensure empty-going races fall back to the default GA prior consistently.

## Issue 6 — WFA adjustment applied inconsistently

| Race | Class | Age | WFA Applied? | WFA Value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SAI R1 | 3 | 3-6yo | No (all 0.0) | Expected ~5 lbs for 3yo in March at 10f |
| SAI R4 | 5 | 3yo | Yes | 2.09 lbs |
| SAI R5 | 5 | 3yo | Yes | 2.09 lbs |

SAI Race 1 is Class 3 with runners aged 3-6yo, but no WFA is applied. Per the WFA table, 3yo runners in March at 2000m (≈10f) should receive approximately **2 lbs**. All runners in SAI R1 show `wfa_adj=0.0`, including the 3yo and 5yo runners.

Looking more carefully: SAI R1 has `horseAge` values of 3-6, which matches the race conditions column. The `wfa_adj` is 0.0 for all — this is because the `horseAge` in the data shows values 3-6 which appear to be race-condition ages rather than individual horse ages. The pipeline may be reading a race-level field rather than per-horse age.

**Recommendation:** Verify that `horseAge` in the output is the individual horse's age and not the race conditions (e.g., "3yo and up").
